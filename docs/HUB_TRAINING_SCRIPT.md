# Añejo HUB — Staff Training Video Script & Storyboard

**What this is:** a shoot-ready script for the Añejo HUB staff-training video. Each scene gives the
**[SCREEN]** action (what to show / click) and the **[VO]** narration (read aloud, word-for-word).
Record the HUB on a phone (it's a mobile-first PWA) for Kitchen/Driver/Vendor modules and on a laptop
for the Owner modules. Total runtime ≈ 22–26 min; each module is self-contained so staff can watch
only the one(s) for their role.

> Production note: shoot against the **sandbox/test** data, never a real customer's PII. Use a test
> staff account per role. Blur any real phone numbers/addresses that appear.

---

## MODULE 0 — Welcome (everyone) · ~1.5 min
- **[SCREEN]** Añejo emblem → slow push-in to the HUB home (`AÑEJO · HUB`).
- **[VO]** "Welcome to the Añejo HUB — the app that runs the kitchen, the deliveries, and the back
  office. If you cook, drive, supply us, or run the show, this is where your day happens. This video
  walks through everything you'll touch. Watch the part for your role; managers, watch it all."
- **[SCREEN]** Quick montage: kitchen order board → driver route map → owner command dashboard.
- **[VO]** "Three things to know up front. One: it's a web app — no install needed, but you can add it
  to your home screen so it opens like a real app. Two: it works on your phone. Three: it remembers
  who you are, so what you see is tailored to your role."

---

## MODULE 1 — Signing in & your account (everyone) · ~2.5 min
- **[SCREEN]** Open `anejocateringco.com/login`. Show the phone-number + PIN fields.
- **[VO]** "To sign in, go to anejocateringco.com slash login. Enter your mobile number and your PIN —
  the owner sets up your account and gives you your PIN in person on your first day."
- **[SCREEN]** Type a test number + PIN → land on the role home.
- **[VO]** "Your PIN is yours. Don't share it. If you forget it, the owner can reset it — you'll be
  asked to choose a new one the next time you sign in."
- **[SCREEN]** Show the "Install Añejo HUB to your home screen" banner → tap **Install**.
- **[VO]** "See this banner? Tap Install to add the HUB to your home screen. Now it's one tap away,
  and it even works offline for the things that matter on a route."
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Account** (`/hub/account`). Show name/role.
- **[VO]** "Your Account page shows your name and role. If it ever says you have no role assigned, ask
  the owner to set you as Kitchen, Driver, or Vendor — that's what unlocks your screens."
- **[SCREEN]** Briefly show the role chips (kitchen / driver / owner / vendor).
- **[VO]** "Quick housekeeping for every role: when you start your shift, clock in. When you finish,
  clock out and file your end-of-day. We'll show you where. Let's get into the roles."

---

## MODULE 2 — Kitchen · ~6 min

### 2.1 Clock in & the day's reminders
- **[SCREEN]** Kitchen home (`AÑEJO · KITCHEN`). Tap the gold **Clock in** button. Show "Today's reminders."
- **[VO]** "Start every shift by clocking in — the gold button at the top. Right below, 'Today's
  reminders' lists anything the owner flagged for the kitchen today. Read it first."

### 2.2 The live order board (the heart of the kitchen)
- **[SCREEN]** Show **Live orders** with the **Today / Upcoming / All** chips, and the **Pending /
  In prep / Ready** columns.
- **[VO]** "This is your command center: Live orders. Use the chips — Today, Upcoming, All — to choose
  what you're looking at. Orders move through three columns: Pending, In prep, and Ready."
- **[SCREEN]** A new order arrives → the **🔔 sound** plays and a card pops into Pending.
- **[VO]** "When a new order comes in, you'll hear a bell and a new card appears in Pending. Tap the
  bell icon to turn the sound on or off — keep it ON during service so nothing slips by."
- **[SCREEN]** Tap a card → **Start prep** (moves to In prep) → **Mark ready** (moves to Ready).
- **[VO]** "Tap a card and hit Start prep when you begin it — it moves to In prep. When the bowls are
  boxed and labeled, tap Mark ready. That's the signal a driver can be dispatched. Marking ready is
  important — it's how the front of the house knows the food actually left your hands."
- **[SCREEN]** Point out per-bowl build details on a card (ingredients/weights).
- **[VO]** "Each card shows the bowls and their builds, so you make exactly what was ordered."

### 2.3 Checklists & food-safety
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Checklists** → run an opening/closing/procedure checklist → **Submit checklist**.
- **[VO]** "Checklists keep us safe and consistent — opening, closing, and prep procedures. Work
  through it, check each item, and submit. It's logged with your name and the time."

### 2.4 Inventory & restock
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Inventory** (`Stock count`). Show **+ Add item**, Name / Unit / Par level / On hand.
- **[VO]** "Inventory is your stock count. Each item has a par level — the amount we want on hand. Update
  'On hand' as you count."
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Restock** → **✨ AI-suggest quantities** → review → **Save draft** → submit to a vendor.
- **[VO]** "When you're low, open Restock. Hit 'AI-suggest quantities' and the system proposes amounts
  based on recent demand. Review them, save the draft, and it goes to the vendor as a purchase order."

### 2.5 Creative Studio (recipe development)
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Studio** (`Creative Studio`) → **Start a session** → capture voice + a photo →
  **✨ AI draft recipe** → **Finalize**.
- **[VO]** "Creative Studio is where new bowls are born. Start a session, talk through your idea, snap
  a photo, and the AI drafts a structured recipe in Añejo's style. When you love it, finalize it — and
  it's ready to add to the rotation."

### 2.6 End of day
- **[SCREEN]** Open the **Daily summary**, then **EOD** → file the end-of-day report → **Clock out**.
- **[VO]** "Before you leave: check the daily summary, file your end-of-day report, and clock out.
  That EOD is what the owner reviews — don't skip it."

## MODULE 3 — Driver · ~6 min

### 3.1 Start of shift
- **[SCREEN]** Driver home (`AÑEJO · DRIVER`) → **Clock in** → show **Today's route** and **Upcoming shifts**.
- **[VO]** "Drivers, clock in to start. Your home screen shows Today's route and your upcoming shifts,
  plus Quick actions for checklists, temp logs, and expenses."

### 3.2 Pre-trip checklist & temp log
- **[SCREEN]** Quick actions → **Checklists** (vehicle/pre-trip) → submit. Then **Temp log** → record a reading.
- **[VO]** "Run your pre-trip checklist, and log your cooler temperature — food safety starts before
  you pull out of the lot."

### 3.3 Kitchen pickup — the bowl-by-bowl gate
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Route** (`AÑEJO · ROUTE`) → **Kitchen pickup** screen: "Tap each bowl as you load it."
- **[VO]** "Here's the most important step. At pickup, you'll see every bowl for your whole route. Tap
  each one as you load it into the vehicle. This is the final check that every bowl actually got made
  and left the kitchen — if something's missing, you'll catch it right here."
- **[SCREEN]** Tap through the bowls → **Start deliveries →** activates.
- **[VO]** "When every bowl is checked, the Start deliveries button lights up. Tap it and you're rolling."

### 3.4 Driving the route, stop by stop
- **[SCREEN]** Show the first stop card: customer first name, address, notes, ETA. Tap **Navigate**.
- **[VO]** "Each stop shows the customer's first name, the address, any drop-off notes, and your ETA.
  Tap Navigate to open turn-by-turn directions. The moment you start driving to a stop, that customer
  automatically gets a text that you're on the way."
- **[SCREEN]** Show the **Arriving soon** button (manual backup) and mention the auto ~10-min text.
- **[VO]** "As you get close — about ten minutes out — the customer gets an 'arriving soon' heads-up.
  That can fire automatically from your location; if you ever need to, tap 'Arriving soon' yourself."

### 3.5 Proof of delivery
- **[SCREEN]** At the door: **Proof photo (take at the door)** → snap photo → **Mark delivered**.
- **[VO]** "At the door, take a proof photo — the bowls where you left them. Tap Mark delivered, and the
  customer gets a 'delivered' text with that photo, plus a quick 'how did we do?' Then the next stop
  loads automatically. Repeat until the route's done."
- **[SCREEN]** Show a failed-delivery path → choose a reason (no answer / wrong address / refused).
- **[VO]** "If a delivery can't be completed, mark it failed and pick the reason. That tells the office
  immediately so they can sort it out."

### 3.6 Issues, mileage, availability, end of day
- **[SCREEN]** Quick actions → **Report issue** (`Type`: complaint / equipment / safety / scheduling) → submit.
- **[VO]** "Hit a problem on the road — a complaint, an equipment or safety issue? Report it from Quick
  actions. It goes straight to the owner."
- **[SCREEN]** **Mileage / Expense** → log miles / an expense with a receipt photo.
- **[VO]** "Log your mileage and any expenses with a photo of the receipt — that's how you get reimbursed."
- **[SCREEN]** When the route's done → toggle **Available** for more orders. Then **EOD** → **Clock out**.
- **[VO]** "Finished your route and free to take more? Flip yourself to Available — the owner can then
  send you an on-demand order. When you're truly done, file your end-of-day and clock out."

## MODULE 4 — Owner / Manager · ~7 min
> Shoot on a laptop. This is the control tower.

### 4.1 Command dashboard
- **[SCREEN]** Owner home (`AÑEJO · COMMAND`): **At a glance**, **Alerts**, **Activity** (Expand/Collapse).
- **[VO]** "Owners land on Command. 'At a glance' is your live pulse — orders, deliveries, staff on
  shift. 'Alerts' surfaces anything needing you: a missing report, a temperature excursion, a failed
  delivery. 'Activity' is the running feed of everything happening — expand it for the full history."

### 4.2 Deliveries & dispatch
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Deliveries** (`AÑEJO · DELIVERIES`). Show **Select a driver**, the unassigned list,
  and the **🛵 on-demand dispatch** banner with 🟢 available-driver markers.
- **[VO]** "Deliveries is your dispatch board. Pick a driver, select the orders, and assign the route —
  it optimizes the stop order and ETAs automatically. When an on-demand order is marked ready, you'll
  see a 'ready for dispatch' prompt and which drivers are available — assign it and it's on its way."

### 4.3 Staff management
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Staff** (`Staff management`): **On shift now**, **EOD compliance today**,
  add a staff member, set role + PIN.
- **[VO]** "Staff is where you add people, set their role, and issue their PIN — remember, six to ten
  digits. You can see who's clocked in right now and who still owes an end-of-day report. Reset a PIN
  here any time; they'll be asked to choose a new one on their next sign-in."

### 4.4 Customers (CRM) & manual orders
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Customers**: search, a customer profile (orders, subscription, plan), **+ New manual order**.
- **[VO]** "Customers is your CRM — every customer, their order history, subscription, and plan in one
  place. Taking an order over the phone? Use 'New manual order' to enter it straight into the kitchen."

### 4.5 Comms
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Comms**: **Open Messages**, **Open tickets**, **All alerts**, filters.
- **[VO]** "Comms is every conversation and issue in one inbox — customer messages, staff and driver
  tickets, and system alerts. Filter, open a thread, and resolve it."

### 4.6 AI Ops
- **[SCREEN]** Open **AI Ops**: **📊 Demand forecast & prep sheet** (Run forecast now), **🤖 AI Suggestions** (Run now).
- **[VO]** "AI Ops does the thinking. Run a demand forecast to get tomorrow's prep sheet. AI Suggestions
  proposes restocks and operational fixes you can accept with one tap. Use it daily — it pays for itself."

### 4.7 Finance
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Finance**: **Window / Days**, **Pending expenses**, **Export report**.
- **[VO]** "Finance rolls up revenue, expenses, and tips by window. Approve pending expenses here, and
  export a report for your bookkeeper whenever you need one."

### 4.8 Schedule
- **[SCREEN]** Open **Schedule** (`This week`): **Add shift** for a driver, cancel a shift.
- **[VO]** "Schedule is your week at a glance. Add shifts per driver, cancel when plans change — and
  those shifts are what each person sees as 'upcoming' on their home screen."

---

## MODULE 5 — Vendor · ~1.5 min
- **[SCREEN]** Vendor home (`AÑEJO · VENDOR`): **Open purchase orders**, **Recently delivered**, **Messages**.
- **[VO]** "Vendors, your screen is simple. 'Open purchase orders' are the restocks the kitchen needs
  from you. Open one, fulfill it, and mark it delivered — that updates the kitchen instantly. 'Messages'
  is your direct line to the owner. That's it — you're set."

---

## CLOSING — everyone · ~0.5 min
- **[SCREEN]** Return to the Añejo emblem.
- **[VO]** "That's the Añejo HUB. Clock in, do great work, keep the food safe, and file your end-of-day.
  The app handles the rest. Questions? Ask the owner — and welcome to the team."

---

## Appendix — quick-reference (print one card per role)
- **Kitchen:** clock in → reminders → work the board (Pending → In prep → Ready, keep 🔔 on) →
  checklists → inventory/restock → studio → EOD → clock out.
- **Driver:** clock in → pre-trip + temp → **tap every bowl at pickup** → Start deliveries → Navigate /
  Arriving soon → **proof photo** → Mark delivered → repeat → Available / EOD → clock out.
- **Owner:** Command (alerts) → Deliveries (dispatch) → Staff → Customers → Comms → AI Ops → Finance → Schedule.
- **Vendor:** open PO → fulfill → mark delivered → Messages.
