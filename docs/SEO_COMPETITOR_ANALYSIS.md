**What the numbers say for Añejo's pricing & positioning:**
- **Price floor is ~$7.50–10/meal at volume.** Añejo's per-meal price should sit in or
  near this band at the subscription tier or it will lose the price-sensitive shopper —
  but the **Cuban + macros** angle lets it justify the *top* of the band rather than
  racing to the $7.50 floor (no rival offers that combination).
- **Reviews are the local-pack currency.** Clean Eatz (4.8★, 65 reviews) and Jet Fuel
  (~69 reviews) rank locally on review volume Añejo starts at zero on. Closing this gap
  is the #1 measurable lever — and it's free (drive reviews off the existing post-delivery
  SMS). Target: ~25–30 reviews to start appearing credibly in the local pack.
- **"$0 delivery on subscription"** (Ideal Nutrition) and **"one-time order"**
  (Fresh Meal Plan) are conversion features worth matching/messaging — free delivery for
  subscribers removes the biggest checkout objection; a no-commitment single order lowers
  first-purchase friction.
- **Storefront ≠ requirement.** Only Clean Eatz/Fitlife have physical locations; the rest
  win delivery-only. Añejo doesn't need a storefront to compete — it needs reviews + GBP.

### The Cuban niche — effectively open
Searching "Cuban meal prep / healthy Cuban bowls delivery" surfaces **recipe blogs**
(Katelyn Nutrition, The Creative Bite, Half Baked Harvest), **not delivery competitors**.
Miami "Cuban food delivery" returns restaurants serving traditional plates — **not
macro-tracked meal prep**. No one in the PBC delivery set leads with Cuban.

> **Takeaway: "Cuban meal prep delivery" is white space.** Añejo's positioning —
> Cuban flavors *plus* tracked macros — has near-zero direct competition. That's the
> wedge. The generic "healthy meal prep WPB" term is crowded and brand-dominated
> (Jet Fuel); the Cuban-intent terms are winnable now.

## What the competitors do that Añejo should match

1. **Per-diet intent pages.** Rivals rank with named plan pages (keto, paleo,
   high-protein, vegan). Añejo has per-**city** and per-**bowl** pages but no per-**diet**
   pages. Add intent pages — "High-Protein Cuban Meal Prep," "Keto-Friendly Cuban Bowls,"
   "Paleo Cuban Meal Prep" — each mapping existing bowls to the diet and cross-linking to
   /order. Low effort, captures diet-qualified searchers the city pages miss.

2. **Recipe/blog funnel.** The Cuban-bowl *recipe* queries are real, high-volume, and
   low-competition (that's literally what ranks today). A short recipe/blog section
   ("Make-at-home Cuban macro bowl" → soft CTA "or let us prep it") captures top-funnel
   traffic the meal-prep pages never will, and earns the kind of informational links that
   lift domain authority.

3. **Social-proof in titles/snippets.** Jet Fuel wins clicks with "voted favorite."
   Once Añejo has reviews, work a credibility hook into the homepage title/meta and add
   `AggregateRating` schema (only when ratings are real — never fabricate).

## The real local battleground: Google Business Profile + reviews
The PBC winners (Jet Fuel, Clean Eatz, Fitlife) dominate the **local pack**, driven by
**Google Business Profile + review velocity**, not just on-page SEO. This is the single
highest-leverage off-site item and it's **owner action, not a code change**:
- Claim/optimize the Google Business Profile (category: Meal delivery service; service
  area: PBC cities already targeted on the landing pages).
- Drive reviews from the first real customers (post-delivery SMS already exists — add a
  review-ask step once GBP is live).
- Keep NAP (name/address/phone) identical between GBP and the site footer/schema.

## Priority order
1. **(Owner)** Google Business Profile + review engine — biggest local-pack lever.
2. **(Build)** Per-diet intent pages (high-protein / keto / paleo Cuban) → own the
   winnable Cuban-intent long tail.
3. **(Build)** Recipe/blog funnel → top-of-funnel + link authority.
4. **(Build, gated on reviews)** AggregateRating schema + credibility hook in meta.
