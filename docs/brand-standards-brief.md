# Añejo Catering Co. — Brand & Standards Brief

**Source of truth for Creative Studio AI, menu development, kitchen execution, packaging, and customer-facing brand standards.**

## 1. Who we are

**Añejo Catering Co. LLC** is a longevity-forward, Mediterranean-Cuban bowl service based in **Palm Beach County, Florida**.

We create high-protein, macro-balanced bowls rooted in clean Mediterranean nutrition, Cuban sazón, premium presentation, and real-life performance. Añejo exists for people who want to live, train, work, and feel better without eating boring "diet food."

Our food should feel fresh, elevated, abundant, flavorful, and intentional.

## 2. Vision & mission

**Vision:**
To build Añejo into South Florida's leading premium longevity food brand — a trusted name in healthy bowls, macro-personalized meal plans, gym partnerships, corporate wellness, and elevated catering. Añejo is being built as a legacy brand: Cuban soul, Mediterranean discipline, premium standards, and food that helps people become the best version of themselves.

**Mission:**
To make healthy food taste so good that people can stay consistent with their goals without feeling restricted. Every bowl should support performance, longevity, and confidence while delivering the flavor, beauty, and care of a premium culinary experience.

**What makes us different:**
Añejo is not basic meal prep. We combine chef-driven Cuban flavor, Mediterranean-inspired nutrition, macro discipline, premium packaging, and goal-based customization. We build food for results, but we present it like a high-end lifestyle brand. The difference is: flavor first, health always, presentation never compromised.

## 3. Our three product lines

### A. À la carte / On-demand bowls / Fit Drinks & Shots

The original launch line.

* Fixed at **16 oz**
* Built to the Golden Rule: **40% protein / 30% carbs / 30% fat**
* Default product anyone can order
* Designed for walk-up, delivery, pickup, gyms, offices, clinics, and general customers
* Nutrition is approximate and stated in ranges

### B. The Macro Portal

Premium subscription service.

* Targets high-end gym trainers and their clients chasing specific goals
* Uses the same Añejo bowl system but adjusts portions to each client's macro targets
* Sold as goal-sized **12-bowl plans**
* The Golden Rule is the starting point, but the client's prescribed macros override it
* Built for transformation, accountability, consistency, and premium trainer partnerships
* *Breakfast line coming soon*

### C. Traditional Cuban Bites for Catering & Events

Chef-driven Cuban bites and platters for catering and events, held to the same ingredient-quality, allergen, food-safety, and presentation standards as the bowl lines.

## 4. The Golden Rule

Every standard 16 oz Añejo bowl targets:

**40% protein / 30% carbs / 30% fat by calories**

When scaling, substituting, or creating new bowls, preserve this ratio unless a Macro Portal client's plan specifies otherwise.

Nutrition must always be presented as **approximate ranges**, never as precise medical claims. Añejo does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease.

## 5. Culinary house style

Añejo's flavor identity is:

* Bright citrus
* Cuban sazón
* Mediterranean freshness
* Chimichurri and house Añejo sauces
* Quinoa-forward and clean grain bases
* High-protein builds
* Anti-inflammatory ingredients where possible
* Generous fiber
* Fresh herbs, acids, spices, seeds, and microgreens
* Sauce served on the side unless a photo or plated sample requires controlled drizzle
* No added sugars or unhealthy oils and fats
* Smoke-infused extra virgin olive oil

The food should taste craveable first, then reveal itself as healthy.

### Texture and temperature standards

* Proteins must be seared, grilled, roasted, or cooked with visible culinary intention — never pale, watery, or steamed-looking.
* Chicken should be sliced cleanly on the bias and look juicy.
* Steak should have color, char, and structure.
* Shrimp should look glossy, firm, and premium.
* Salmon and tuna should be the hero visually and nutritionally.
* Greens must stay crisp and never soggy.
* Vegetables should be bright, clean-cut, and sectioned.
* Bases should be fluffy, not wet or compressed.
* Seeds, nuts, microgreens, and toppings are used to create texture and premium finish.

### Signature techniques and ingredients

Añejo is known for:

* Clockwise sectional bowl plating
* Hero protein placement from approximately 5 o'clock to 7 o'clock
* Controlled sauce identity
* Microgreens as a premium finishing signature or main salad
* Citrus-forward sauces
* Cuban-inspired seasoning layered into clean ingredients
* Mediterranean structure with Cuban flavor memory
* Premium abundance without messy overcrowding

## 7. Ingredient & sourcing standards

### Quality bar

Añejo ingredients must look, taste, and perform like premium food.

* Fresh produce whenever possible
* Local sourcing when quality, consistency, and pricing allow
* Organic where it matters most and is operationally realistic
* Proteins must be high quality and handled carefully
* No cheap-looking fillers
* No watery vegetables
* No wilted greens
* No bland "meal prep" execution
* Every ingredient must earn its place visually, nutritionally, and culinarily

### Preferred ingredients

* Quinoa and clean grain bases
* Lean proteins
* Salmon, tuna, shrimp, chicken, steak, tofu, pork loin
* Greens, cucumber, tomatoes, avocado, mango, beans, chickpeas, roasted vegetables
* Citrus, herbs, garlic, olive-oil-based sauces, chimichurri-style sauces
* Seeds, almonds, pepitas, sesame, microgreens
* Anti-inflammatory spices such as turmeric where appropriate

### Ingredients to avoid or restrict

* Excess added sugar
* Heavy seed-oil-forward cooking
* Greasy sauces
* Overly processed ingredients
* Artificial-tasting dressings
* Cheap fillers used only to increase volume
* Ingredients that make the bowl look wet, sloppy, or low-end

Portioning for each standard bowl is **16 oz**. Exact ingredient weights come from the kitchen manual and production tickets.

## 8. Allergens & dietary rules

Allergen discipline is non-negotiable.

### Hard allergen rules

* Allergens must be clearly identified on recipes, labels, menus, and production sheets.
* Always label or disclose: nuts, tree nuts, sesame, egg, dairy, gluten/wheat, fish, shellfish, and soy when present or possible through supplier cross-contact.
* Never assume a customer "can probably have it."
* Never suggest substitutions that conflict with a recorded allergy or preference.
* If a client has recorded allergens, those override flavor, presentation, and general menu standards.
* Sauce and toppings must be checked separately, not just the main bowl.
* Nuts and seeds must be removable where possible, but cross-contact must still be disclosed if the kitchen is not fully allergen-free.

### Current Añejo-specific allergen notes

* Croquetas are not béchamel-based.
* Croqueta masa is all-purpose flour-based.
* Croquetas do not contain milk/dairy in the masa unless the specific item contains cheese.
* Breaded items may use egg wash.
* No soy is intentionally added unless a specific supplier ingredient contains it.
* Only items containing cheese or dairy-based sauces should be labeled as dairy-containing.
* Supplier cross-contact risk must still be acknowledged.

### Cross-contamination standards

* Use clean gloves, utensils, boards, and containers for allergen-sensitive orders.
* Separate nut/seed toppings from nut-free orders.
* Keep sauces labeled and closed.
* Package allergen-sensitive orders separately.
* Mark modified orders clearly on production tickets and labels.
* When in doubt, disclose instead of guessing.

## 9. Food-safety & cold-chain standards

Cold-chain and food safety are operational law.

* Coolers must read **≤ 41°F** at loadout and dropoff.
* Record temperatures at both ends.
* Hot items must be held at **≥ 135°F**.
* Follow Kitchen Opening, sanitation, batch labeling, cooling, storage, and delivery-safety procedures in the Standards Library.
* Label batches with production date, item, handler, and shelf-life.
* Standard shelf life is **3 days** unless the kitchen manual states otherwise.
* Any item outside safe temperature, quality, or visual standard must not be served.

## 10. Plating & presentation

A correct Añejo bowl should look premium before the customer takes a bite.

### Signature bowl layout

* Use clockwise sectional plating.
* The protein is the hero and usually sits from **5 o'clock to 7 o'clock**.
* The base should support the bowl without hiding the premium ingredients.
* Vegetables are arranged in clean, intentional sections.
* Avocado, mango, tomatoes, cucumber, greens, beans, chickpeas, roasted vegetables, and toppings should each have visual space.
* Microgreens finish every premium bowl unless unavailable or inappropriate for the specific build.
* Seeds, nuts, sesame, pepitas, or coconut are used as controlled texture accents.
* The bowl should look abundant but clean — never empty, never chaotic.

### Photo standard

* Full round bowl visible.
* Bowl front and center.
* Premium matte black, dark forest green, cream, or neutral backgrounds.
* Lighting should feel luxury, editorial, and fresh.
* Ingredients must look bright and high-quality.
* Sauce may be shown as a controlled drizzle for photography, but operationally sauce is usually served on the side.
* No messy edges.
* No overfilled lids.
* No soggy greens.
* No cheap takeout look.

### Packaging standard

Añejo packaging should feel more premium than ordinary delivery food.

* Clean bowl presentation
* Round 3-inch lid label when used
* Bowl name clearly visible
* Client name where possible
* QR code may link to bowl breakdown, macros, and review request
* Sauce cup should be labeled or visually paired with the bowl
* Delivery should feel like receiving a premium wellness product, not just lunch

## 11. Brand voice

### Tone

Añejo speaks with warmth, confidence, and expertise.

The voice is:

* Premium but not arrogant
* Health-smart but not clinical
* Flavor-first but disciplined
* Cuban-rooted but modern
* Confident but never fake
* Emotional when telling the founder story
* Clean and direct when describing food

Añejo should never sound like generic meal prep, gym bro food, or hospital food.

### Naming convention

Bowls use bold, memorable Spanish names that feel strong, clean, and brandable.

Examples:

* COCO
* LIGERO
* CONGREEN
* FUEGO
* VIDA
* MAR
* RAÍZ
* FUERZA

Names should be short, iconic, easy to say, and emotionally connected to the bowl's identity.

### Description style

Food descriptions should be sensory and benefit-driven without making medical claims.

Use language like:

* Fresh
* Balanced
* Protein-forward
* Citrus-bright
* Clean
* Craveable
* Goal-aligned
* Omega-rich
* Fiber-rich
* Chef-built
* Cuban-inspired
* Mediterranean-rooted
* Premium wellness bowl

### Words and claims we avoid

Avoid:

* "Diet food"
* "Weight loss guaranteed"
* "Cures"
* "Treats"
* "Prevents disease"
* "Medical meal"
* "Doctor-approved" unless actually documented
* "Exact macros" when using estimates
* "No risk" allergen language
* Cheap hype such as "best ever" without context
* Overpromising transformation results

## 12. Non-negotiables

The AI, kitchen, and brand must always honor the following:

1. Standard à la carte bowls are **16 oz**.
2. Standard bowls follow the Golden Rule: **40% protein / 30% carbs / 30% fat** unless a Macro Portal plan overrides it.
3. Macro Portal client targets override general culinary defaults.
4. Allergens override everything.
5. Nutrition is always approximate and never presented as a medical claim.
6. Sauce is served on the side unless a specific photo, event, or plated sample requires controlled drizzle.
7. Every bowl must look premium, abundant, clean, and intentional.
8. Microgreens are part of the Añejo premium visual signature whenever available.
9. No bowl should look like basic meal prep.
10. Cuban flavor and Mediterranean nutrition must work together — never one without the other.
11. Packaging must reinforce the premium experience.
12. The brand must protect trust: honest ingredients, clean execution, safe handling, clear labeling, and no exaggerated claims.
13. Añejo is built for legacy — every recipe, photo, label, sauce, bowl, delivery, and customer interaction should feel like it belongs to a brand that can scale.
