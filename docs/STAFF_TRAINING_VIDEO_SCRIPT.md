# Añejo HUB — Staff Training Video Script & Storyboard

**Purpose:** teach every Añejo staff member how to sign in and use the HUB for their role.
**Format:** screen-recording with voiceover. Each scene lists `[SCREEN]` (what to show),
`[DO]` (what to click), and `[VO]` (narration). Record at the live site, signed in as a test
user per role. Total runtime ≈ 14–18 min; the role chapters are standalone so you can also cut
short, role-specific clips (Kitchen-only, Driver-only, etc.).

**Brand:** keep the Añejo look — matte black / forest green / cream / gold. Calm, confident,
warm voice. Avoid jargon; staff are line cooks and drivers, not engineers.

---

## CHAPTER 0 — Cold open & sign-in (everyone) · ~1.5 min

**Scene 0.1 — Title**
- `[SCREEN]` Añejo wordmark on dark green; subtitle "The HUB — your back office."
- `[VO]` "Welcome to the Añejo HUB — the one place we run the business. Whatever your
  role — kitchen, delivery, or owner — this is where your day lives. Let's get you in."

**Scene 0.2 — Install as an app**
- `[SCREEN]` Phone browser at `anejocateringco.com/login`; the "Add to Home Screen" prompt.
- `[DO]` Add to home screen; open the installed icon.
- `[VO]` "First time on your phone: add the HUB to your home screen. It opens like a real app,
  works offline-friendly, and is how you'll get notifications. Do this once."

**Scene 0.3 — Sign in with your PIN**
- `[SCREEN]` `/login` — the unified sign-in: identifier field, then PIN pad.
- `[DO]` Type your phone or email → Continue → enter your 6-digit PIN.
- `[VO]` "Everyone has their own login — never share it. Enter your phone or email, then your
  personal PIN. First time, you'll set a new PIN you'll remember. Five wrong tries locks the
  account for fifteen minutes, so go slow."
- `[ON-SCREEN TEXT]` "Your PIN = your signature. The HUB records who did what."

**Scene 0.4 — The bottom bar**
- `[SCREEN]` Any role home; highlight the fixed bottom navigation bar.
- `[VO]` "Wherever you are, the bar at the bottom switches between your pages. It never moves —
  no scrolling to find it. Let's start with your role."

## CHAPTER 1 — Kitchen · ~4–5 min

**Scene 1.1 — Clock in & the morning briefing**
- `[SCREEN]` Kitchen home; tap the green "Clock in" card.
- `[DO]` Clock in. A gold "☀️ Good morning" banner appears at the top.
- `[VO]` "Start every shift by clocking in. The first cook to clock in triggers Añejo Ops'
  morning briefing — today's bowl count, the lunch/dinner split, and anything low on stock.
  Read it before you start prepping."

**Scene 1.2 — The order board**
- `[SCREEN]` Kitchen board: columns **Pending → Prep → Ready**. Point out a card with a gold
  **"Subscription"** badge and the lunch/dinner badge.
- `[VO]` "Orders flow left to right: Pending, in Prep, then Ready. A gold 'Subscription' tag
  means it's a member's daily fresh bowl. The badges tell you the day and whether it's lunch or
  dinner. New orders chime."

**Scene 1.3 — Read a bowl's customization**
- `[SCREEN]` Expand an order card → per-bowl detail: bowl name, **size (oz) + size %**, macros,
  the build list with weights, **removals**, **add-ons** (e.g. "+ ½ avocado"), and notes.
- `[VO]` "Tap an order to see every bowl in full: the exact size scaled to that member's macros,
  the ingredient build with weights, anything removed, any add-ons, and special notes. Make
  exactly what's on the card — the weights matter."

**Scene 1.4 — Start prep & check off bowls (PIN)**
- `[SCREEN]` Tap **Start prep** on a Pending order → it moves to Prep. Each bowl shows a
  check-off control. Tap a bowl → **PIN prompt** → "✓ by [name]".
- `[VO]` "Tap Start prep to claim the order. As you finish each bowl, check it off — the HUB
  asks for your PIN, so we always know who made what. That check also gets re-confirmed by the
  driver later, so it has to be honest."

**Scene 1.5 — Mark Ready (PIN, gated)**
- `[SCREEN]` Try **Mark Ready** with a bowl unchecked → blocked. Check the last bowl → Mark
  Ready → PIN → card moves to Ready.
- `[VO]` "You can't mark an order Ready until every bowl is checked off — that's on purpose.
  When it's all done, Mark Ready, confirm your PIN, and it's set out for the driver."

**Scene 1.6 — Inventory & restock**
- `[SCREEN]` Bottom bar → look at **Inventory** (items, on-hand, par, vendor, **unit cost**) and
  the low-stock highlights; then **Restock**.
- `[DO]` Do a quick **count** on one item; show a below-par item flagged.
- `[VO]` "Keep counts current — tap an item and update on-hand. Anything below par turns red and
  feeds the owner's vendor-order suggestions. If you set the unit cost and vendor, Ops can even
  estimate what a restock will cost."

**Scene 1.7 — Checklists, temps, library, Creative Studio**
- `[SCREEN]` Quickly tour **Checklists** (opening/closing/sanitation), **Library** (recipes &
  manuals), and **Studio**.
- `[VO]` "Checklists keep us food-safe — open and close the kitchen by them. The Library has
  every recipe and manual. And Creative Studio is where we develop new bowls with the AI
  sous-chef — it knows our brand, our standards, and the Golden Rule."

**Scene 1.8 — End of day**
- `[SCREEN]` **EOD** → submit the end-of-day report; clock out.
- `[VO]` "Before you leave, file your EOD and clock out. If you forget, the owner gets nudged —
  so just do it."

## CHAPTER 2 — Delivery driver · ~4 min

**Scene 2.1 — Accept or deny a delivery order**
- `[SCREEN]` Driver gets a push + text "New delivery order — accept or deny." Open the app →
  Route screen shows **Accept order / Deny**.
- `[DO]` Tap **Accept order**.
- `[VO]` "When the owner assigns you a run, you'll get a notification and a text. Open the app
  and either Accept or Deny. If you deny — or don't answer within two minutes — it automatically
  goes to the next available driver. Every accept and deny is on your record, so be reliable."

**Scene 2.2 — Kitchen pickup: confirm every bowl**
- `[SCREEN]` Route → **Kitchen pickup** gate: each stop lists its bowls with tappable units.
- `[DO]` Tap each bowl as you load it → **Confirm pickup**. Show a short count flagging the
  kitchen.
- `[VO]` "Before you leave, load and confirm every bowl — tap each one as it goes in the bag.
  You're re-confirming what the kitchen prepped. If something's short, flag it; the kitchen gets
  alerted right away. You can't start deliveries until pickup is confirmed."

**Scene 2.3 — Navigate & 'on the way'**
- `[SCREEN]` Current stop card with ETA; **Navigate** button.
- `[DO]` Tap **Navigate** → it opens maps and texts the customer "on the way."
- `[VO]` "Tap Navigate for each stop — it opens directions and automatically tells that customer
  you're on the way. Work the stops top to bottom."

**Scene 2.4 — Mark delivered (photo)**
- `[SCREEN]` **Mark delivered** sheet → take a proof photo, or check "Couldn't take a photo."
- `[DO]` Snap the photo → Mark delivered → card advances to the next stop.
- `[VO]` "At the door, take a quick proof photo and Mark delivered. The customer gets a text
  that it's dropped. The app jumps you to the next stop automatically. No photo? Check the box
  and it still completes."

**Scene 2.5 — Problems, temps, expenses, EOD**
- `[SCREEN]` Show **Report a problem** (no answer / wrong address / refused), **Temp** log,
  **Expenses** (fuel/receipt photo), **EOD**.
- `[VO]` "If a drop fails, Report a problem and pick the reason — the owner sees it instantly.
  Log cooler temps when asked, snap fuel and expense receipts under Expenses, and finish with
  your EOD. Complete the route when every stop is done."

## CHAPTER 3 — Comms & your account (everyone) · ~1 min

- `[SCREEN]` **Comms** inbox — threads, unread counts; open one and reply.
- `[VO]` "Comms is our group line. The owner posts routes and updates here; you can reply.
  Unread counts show on the bar."
- `[SCREEN]` **Account** — your name, role, change PIN; enable notifications.
- `[VO]` "Under Account, turn on notifications so you never miss an order, and change your PIN
  any time. Switch English or Spanish with the toggle — the whole app follows."

## CHAPTER 4 — Owner command center · ~3–4 min (owner/manager only)

**Scene 4.1 — Overview**
- `[SCREEN]` Owner home: today's tiles (orders, deliveries, alerts), the Activity feed (collapsed
  with "Expand").
- `[VO]` "The Overview is your morning glance — orders, deliveries, open alerts. The Activity
  feed shows everything happening; it stays compact so your menu's always reachable."

**Scene 4.2 — Deliveries: assign a route**
- `[SCREEN]` **Deliveries** → unassigned orders + drivers; build a route, pick a driver, assign.
  Show the badge change to "awaiting driver," and "unfilled — reassign" if everyone passes.
- `[VO]` "Build routes here. Pick the stops and a driver, and assign — that sends the offer.
  You'll see 'awaiting driver,' then it clears when they accept. If nobody takes it, it shows
  'unfilled' and you reassign."

**Scene 4.3 — Customers & subscriptions**
- `[SCREEN]` **Customers** → open a member → profile, orders, subscription, and the **upcoming
  daily prep schedule**.
- `[VO]` "Customers is your CRM. Open anyone to see their orders and subscription, including the
  exact daily bowls we'll deliver this week. Guests can be onboarded into full members here."

**Scene 4.4 — Staff: PINs, reliability, vendor lead times**
- `[SCREEN]` **Staff** → add a member (shows the one-time PIN), set role/team, driver
  reliability (accepted/denied/missed), vendor **lead-days** field.
- `[VO]` "Add staff here — they get a one-time PIN to set their own. You can see each driver's
  reliability, and for vendors, set their lead time so Ops can time orders."

**Scene 4.5 — Añejo Ops (the AI agent)**
- `[SCREEN]` **AI Ops** → Demand forecast & prep sheet, AI Suggestions (vendor order with
  **Accept / Dismiss**), Reports & insights, forecast accuracy, backups.
- `[VO]` "AI Ops is your analyst. It forecasts tomorrow's volume and builds the prep sheet,
  prepares vendor orders with cost and timing for you to Accept or Dismiss, and writes your
  daily standup, end-of-service checks, and weekly summary. Important: it never spends a dollar
  on its own — every order waits for you to confirm. And it tracks how accurate its forecasts
  are so you can trust it over time."
- `[ON-SCREEN TEXT]` "Añejo Ops recommends. You approve. Nothing auto-spends."

**Scene 4.6 — Finance, Kitchen view, Schedule, Content**
- `[SCREEN]` Quick passes: **Finance** (sales, tips, payouts), **Kitchen** (owner's view of the
  board), **Schedule** (driver shifts), **Content** (brand brief, docs).
- `[VO]` "Finance shows sales, tips, and payouts. Schedule sets driver shifts. And Content holds
  the brand brief that powers Creative Studio — keep it current and the AI stays on-brand."

- `[VO]` "Three things to remember: your PIN is your signature — keep it private. Check off your

## Recording checklist
- Record on a phone (the HUB is mobile-first) at `anejocateringco.com`.
- Use **test accounts** per role — never real customer data on camera.
- Blur any real customer name/address/phone in post.
- Keep each chapter as its own clip so you can hand a driver just Chapter 2, a cook Chapters 0–1
  and 3, etc.
- Record an **English** and a **Spanish** voiceover (the UI already toggles ES).
